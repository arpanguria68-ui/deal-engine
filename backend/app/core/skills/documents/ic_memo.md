# Investment Committee Memo — Domain Skill

<correct_patterns>

## Standard IC Memo Structure

### I. Executive Summary (1 page)
- Company description in 3 sentences (what, who, scale)
- Deal type and structure (buyout, growth equity, minority stake)
- Enterprise Value, Equity Investment, implied multiples
- **Clear Recommendation**: PROCEED / PASS / CONDITIONAL PROCEED
- Headline returns: Base case IRR and MOIC
- Top 3 risks with single-line mitigant for each

### II. Company Overview (1-2 pages)
- Business model: Products/services, how the company makes money
- Revenue breakdown by segment/geography if applicable
- Customer concentration (top 5 customers + % of revenue)
- Competitive positioning: Market share, moat analysis
- Key management: CEO, CFO tenure and track record

### III. Market & Industry (1 page)
- TAM: Total Addressable Market ($X billion by 20XX)
- SAM: Serviceable Addressable Market (company's realistic reach)
- Market growth rate (CAGR X% per year, source it)
- Competitive landscape: 3-4 key competitors and positioning
- Secular tailwinds / headwinds

### IV. Financial Analysis (2-3 pages)
- Revenue, EBITDA, FCF for last 3 years + LTM
- Gross margin, EBITDA margin, FCF margin trends
- Quality of Earnings (QoE) adjustments (one-time items, add-backs)
- Working capital dynamics (DSO, DPO, DIO)
- CapEx intensity and maintenance vs growth CapEx split

### V. Investment Thesis (3-5 pillars)
Each pillar should have:
- Clear statement (e.g., "Market share gain in fragmented industry")
- Supporting evidence (data, customer references)
- Value creation mechanism within the hold period

### VI. Deal Terms & Structure
- Enterprise Value and implied EV/EBITDA and EV/Revenue multiples
- Financing: Senior Debt, Sub Debt, Equity mix
- Sources & Uses summary table
- Key legal terms (management rollover, ratchet, governance rights)

### VII. Returns Analysis
- Base, Upside, Downside scenario table: IRR, MOIC, Entry/Exit multiples
- Key assumptions driving each scenario
- Sensitivity: IRR sensitivity to entry/exit multiple and EBITDA growth

### VIII. Risk Factors & Mitigants
Rank risks by Severity (H/M/L) and Likelihood (H/M/L):
| Risk | Severity | Likelihood | Mitigant |
|------|----------|-----------|---------|
| Customer concentration >30% | High | Medium | Contract renewals secured |
| ... | ... | ... | ... |

Flag any deal-breaker risks explicitly.

### IX. Recommendation
- Clear and decisive: PROCEED or PASS or CONDITIONAL PROCEED
- If conditional: specific conditions that must be met
- Proposed next steps and timeline

</correct_patterns>

<common_mistakes>
- ❌ Hiding risks; IC members will find them and lose trust in the author
- ❌ Only showing bull case; always present honest bear case
- ❌ Sources & Uses table does not balance (Total Sources ≠ Total Uses)
- ❌ Returns math inconsistent with deal terms in other sections
- ❌ Generic investment thesis (e.g., "growing market") without specific evidence
- ❌ Vague recommendation — always make a clear PROCEED / PASS call
- ❌ Missing management assessment (quality of leadership is critical to IC)
</common_mistakes>
