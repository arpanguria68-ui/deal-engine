# Market Research & Competitive Analysis — Domain Skill

<correct_patterns>

## Market Sizing Framework (TAM → SAM → SOM)
Always define all three levels:

```
TAM (Total Addressable Market)
  = Total revenue AVAILABLE if 100% market share
  Method: Top-down (industry reports) AND bottom-up validation

SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)
  = TAM × % that matches company's product/geography/segment

SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)
  = SAM × Realistic market share in 3-5 years
  Sanity check: SOM should align with company's revenue projections
```

**Always show the calculation**, not just the final number:
- State the source (e.g., "Per Gartner 2024, global CRM market is $XX billion")
- Show the filter logic to go from TAM → SAM → SOM

## Market Growth Rate (CAGR)
```
CAGR = (End Value / Start Value)^(1/Years) - 1
```
- Include 3-5 year historical CAGR and 3-5 year projected CAGR
- Cite growth drivers: regulatory tailwinds, technology shifts, demographic trends

## Competitive Landscape Analysis

### Porter's Five Forces (Brief Assessment)
Assess each force as: **Low / Medium / High** with 1-sentence rationale:
1. **Threat of New Entrants**: What are the barriers? (CapEx, regulation, switching costs)
2. **Supplier Power**: Are there few suppliers for key inputs?
3. **Buyer Power**: Can customers easily switch or negotiate?
4. **Threat of Substitutes**: Are there alternative products/services?
5. **Competitive Rivalry**: How many players, and how intense is price competition?

<common_mistakes>
- ❌ Stating TAM without a source or calculation (always cite data)
- ❌ Making TAM unrealistically large (a $500B TAM for a $5M revenue company is a red flag)
- ❌ Bottom-up market sizing that doesn't reconcile with top-down estimates
- ❌ Generic competitive landscape without naming actual companies
- ❌ No discussion of switching costs (critical for evaluating defensibility)
- ❌ CAGR stated without a time period
</common_mistakes>
